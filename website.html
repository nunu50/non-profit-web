About Islam

Islam is a monotheistic religion founded in the 7th century CE by the Prophet Muhammad. It is based on the teachings of the Quran, which Muslims believe to be the word of God. Islam has over 1.8 billion followers worldwide and is one of the fastest-growing religions.

Islam is centered around five fundamental acts of worship known as the Five Pillars of Islam. These pillars form the foundation of a Muslim's faith and practice.

Shahada

The Shahada is the declaration of faith in Islam. It states: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is his messenger."

Salah

Salah is the ritual prayer performed five times a day by Muslims. It is a way of communicating with Allah.

Zakat

Zakat is the act of giving a portion of one's wealth to charity, helping those in need.

Sawm

Sawm is the fasting done during the holy month of Ramadan, where Muslims abstain from food and drink from dawn to sunset.

Hajj

Hajj is the pilgrimage to the holy city of Mecca that Muslims make at least once in their lifetime, if they are physically and financially able.
